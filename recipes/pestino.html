Pestiños

Transport your taste buds to the sun-kissen regions of Spain with Pestiños, a delightful treat deeply rooted in Andalusian culinary tradition.

These honey-coated pastries are fried to golden perfection, boasting a perfect blend of citrus and anise flavors.

Pestiños are a cherished indulgence, often enjoyed during festive occasions, making them a sweet sensation worth savoring

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of one orange
  • 1 teaspoon anise seeds, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

For frying:

  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Honey Syrup

  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, olive oil, white wine, orange juice, orange zest, crushed anise seeds, ground cinnamon, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  4. On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thickness.
  5. Cut the dough into diamond shapes or rectangles.
  6. In a deep pan, heat vegetable oil for frying to 350˚F (175˚C)
  7. Fry the pestiños in batches until they are golden brown on both sides. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  8. In a small saucepan, combine honey, water, and a cinnamon stick. Heat over low heat until the honey is melted and the mixture is well combined.
  9. Dip each fried pestiño into the honey syrup, ensuring it is coated evenly. Place them on a wire rack to allow excess syrup to drip off.
  10. Allow the pestiños to cool and the honey coating to set.
  11. Serve these sweet delights and savor the essence of Andalusian festivities.

Enjoy the sweet, aromatic bliss of Pestiños as they bring the warmth and flavors of Spanish tradition to your table.
